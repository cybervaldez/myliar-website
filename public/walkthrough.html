1.0 · WHAT THIS GAME ACTUALLY IS

It's a daily-loop "life-RPG" where four characters live in your phone and write narratives about your day. The pitch sounds like an AI gimmick but the execution is closer to a Persona / Mystic Messenger hybrid: the writing is curated for the first seven days (the "Main Line"), then opens up.

Each in-game day is 5–7 events. Each event is a short scene + three choices. The third choice is always chaotic (rolls a die). Stats move, REL with the focal character moves, sometimes you get an item.

The thing that distinguishes it from other AI-companion apps is the locked-canon discipline: the four canonical characters (Sam, Hana, Kenji, Mei) cannot be edited by the player. You can chat with them, toggle which memories of you they keep in their head, and delete memories — but you can't reshape who they are. There's a separate "Elseworld" tier for fully-customizable LLM-generated characters; that's not in this walkthrough.

Save discipline: The Main Line save lives on your device by default. After Day 3 the game offers an opt-in email-OTP sync (game state only — never chats or journal). If you don't opt in, your save dies with your phone. Day 4 morning is the only re-nudge.

2.0 · CONTROLS PRIMER

The three-choice trichotomy

Every event ends in (A), (B), (C). The roles are fixed:

RoleBest forWhat you give up
LOGICAL (A) Stat farming. Steady REL gain. Most items drop here. Variance. You'll never have a story to tell about playing logical-only.
PASSIVE (B) Information without commitment. Sometimes free observations. REL goes down on most days (-1 standard). The character clocks you.
CHAOTIC (C) 🎲 Items on crit-success. Funny memories. Highest character payoff. Crit-fail is real (~40% baseline). Some chaotics are REL-negative even on success.

The dice math (chaotic only)

Chaotic choices roll a die with a baseline 40% crit-fail rate. On success: delta multiplied ×2, item drop (if any). On crit-fail: delta multiplied ×-1 (so positive deltas become negative, REL gets hit hard, no item).

Strategy: The chaotic option is the right pick when the upside is what you want. If a chaotic gives CHR +1 / REL -2 on success, the math says you'll average roughly CHR +1 / REL -2.8 across many rolls (since crit-fail makes it WORSE). Don't chaotic-spam for stats; chaotic for the story.

Inquiries

Some events have a "[?]" button. Tap before deciding. The character explains something in their own voice. Free. Doesn't cost AP, doesn't burn a choice. If you ignore inquiries you'll miss about 30% of the game's worldbuilding.

6.0 · TIER-UP REVEALS

Heavy spoilers below. Three reveals, one per character. The fourth (Sam) is implied but not in canon.

Day 1 evt-6 — Hana, MEMORY: the brother

The shoes are her brother's. He was her coach. He's not around anymore. She runs at 5 AM for him.

Why it works: by Day 1 evt-6 Hana has been intense for six events. The reveal isn't an exposition dump — it re-contextualizes the 5 AM texts as a ritual rather than a hectoring habit. The "he was her coach" line lands BECAUSE the prior six events established that she IS a coach.

Day 4 evt-4 — Kenji, OPINION: respect for the lie

He knew the raccoon story was a lie. He filed it anyway. He says explicitly that he prefers people who negotiate the column edges over people who walk every line.

Why it works: it's the only tier-up that retroactively makes a chaotic crit-success ALSO an emotional payoff. The game is telling you: chaos is canon. The mechanics support the theme.

Day 5 evt-5 — Mei, FEAR: the stove fire

She used to have a different stove in a different kitchen. There was a fire. She counts everything since then partly because she's afraid she'll forget something and the kitchen will burn.

Why it works: best-paced reveal in the canon. Three sentences of cold-open shift (rhythm breaks, looks at her hand, looks at the stove) before she speaks. The line "the count keeps me safe from the heat" retroactively recodes the 2cm spacing and the August-11th paste and the inspection list as trauma response. It changes what you've been watching for the last 40 minutes.

Day 7 evt-4 — Kenji, MEMORY: the receipt raccoon

Kenji has been folding origami raccoons out of the raccoon-week receipts since Day 4. He gives one to you when you admit more lies. It's the only physical artifact in the canon that ONE of the characters made WITH THEIR OWN HANDS, for you.

Why it works: surveillance-as-care, made literal. The audit was the relationship. The folded paper proves he was paying attention to the small things you said weren't real.

The four reveals rotate categories: MEMORY · (none) · (none) · OPINION · FEAR · (none) · MEMORY. Per the game's rotation rule, Kenji's second tier-up is memory-flavored because he already did opinion on Day 4. The QUESTION category is unused in the canon — saved for the post-Day-7 expansion.

STR (Hana's lane)

Most events with STR deltas are Hana days (1, 2, 3, 6). Optimal: logical-A on every Hana logical-with-STR. Floor: STR +4. Ceiling: STR +8 (if you take chaotic-crit-success on Day 6 evt-2 strip-and-march).

CHR (chaos lane)

Every chaotic choice in the week has a CHR delta. Crit-fails turn positive into negative, but the EV is still net positive on most days. Pure CHR run: +18 to +24 across the week.

  2. The August 11th paste is the only multi-day trophy. If you defended the paste on Day 5 evt-3 chaotic, the jar appears in the background of Mei's grocery-list scene at evt-6. The label "TEST SUBJECT — DO NOT EAT" is still in her handwriting. I missed this on run 1 because I picked logical at evt-3.
  3. The day-4 raccoon arc pays off twice. If you DO NOT pick chaotic on Day 4 evt-3, the Day 7 evt-4 reveal still happens but Kenji refers to "the discrepancy" instead of the raccoon specifically. The Origami Receipt Raccoon item is gated on having picked chaotic on Day 4. Do the raccoon.
  4. Inquiries that change the next day. Day 6 evt-4's "comfort is the slowest poison" inquiry — that exact phrase is in Hana's Day 3 memory write. The game is showing you that her language is consistent across days, which means the LLM context is being threaded properly. Small craft, but it's there.
  5. Kenji is funded by Hana. Day 7 evt-1's "Hana's physical requirements are a recurring column I have been quietly funding without comment" is the only canonical mention that Kenji is paying Hana's expenses. I missed this entirely on run 1.

9.0 · MINI-REVIEW

What's working

The character voice is the strongest part of the game. I covered the speaker labels on a printed-out transcript and I could identify Hana from Kenji from Mei in three lines or fewer, every time. That's craft discipline. The dialogue rule the team calls "blind-test" is real.

The tier-up cadence is also working. Three reveals in seven days, each in a different category (memory / opinion / fear / memory), each landing at evt-(N-1) of its day. The cadence has the rhythm of a Persona Social Link arc compressed into a week.

The chaos discipline is the load-bearing design choice. Chaotic is not "the wrong answer that does damage." It's the answer that gets you the memory. Day 3 evt-5 (eat the agreement card), Day 4 evt-3 (raccoon fraud), Day 5 evt-5 (deflect Mei's fear via Hana) all reward the disrespect with something only chaos can deliver. The game means it.

What's still wobbly

Day 2 is the connecting tissue and it shows. The Kenji-call seam at evt-2 is the only thing that earns the day. If your first run had a crit-fail on evt-3 chaotic AND you picked logical or passive on evt-2, Day 2 is the day you might bounce.

The GLD stat exists in the UI but doesn't move in canon. Sam acknowledges this if asked, which honors the cosmology, but a player who notices will wonder.

The Elseworld tier (not covered here — separate walkthrough) is a huge surface that the Main Line doesn't introduce. New players who hit the picker after Day 7 may not know what just happened. Documentation gap, not a story gap.

Verdict

9/10 for what it's trying to do. The thing it's trying to do isn't what most other "AI companion" apps are doing — those are trying to be ChatGPT With a Persona. This is trying to be a published RPG module that happens to be authored by an LLM under a tight gate. The discipline of the gate is what makes it work. If the gate slips in v1.0+, the game dies. If it doesn't, this is the only product of its kind I can name.

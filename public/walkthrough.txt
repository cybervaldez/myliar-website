===============================================================================
[1.00]  WHAT THIS GAME ACTUALLY IS
===============================================================================

It's a daily-loop "life-RPG" where four characters live in your phone and
write narratives about your day. The pitch sounds like an AI gimmick but
the execution is closer to a Persona / Mystic Messenger hybrid: the
writing is curated for the first seven days (the "Main Line"), then
opens up.

Each in-game day is 5-7 events. Each event is a short scene + three
choices. The third choice is always chaotic (rolls a die). Stats move,
REL with the focal character moves, sometimes you get an item.

The thing that distinguishes it from other AI-companion apps is the
*locked-canon discipline*: the four canonical characters (Sam, Hana,
Kenji, Mei) cannot be edited by the player. You can chat with them,
toggle which memories of you they keep in their head, and delete
memories -- but you can't reshape who they are. There's a separate
"Elseworld" tier for fully-customizable LLM-generated characters; that's
not in this walkthrough.

+-----------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| > NOTE -- SAVE DISCIPLINE                                                   |
|                                                                             |
| The Main Line save lives on your device by default. After Day 3 the game    |
| offers an opt-in email-OTP sync (game state only -- never chats or          |
| journal). If you don't opt in, your save dies with your phone. Day 4        |
| morning is the only re-nudge.                                               |

===============================================================================
[2.00]  CONTROLS PRIMER
===============================================================================

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[2.01]  The three-choice trichotomy
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Every event ends in (A), (B), (C). The roles are fixed:

  [A] LOGICAL  -- Best for: stat farming. Steady REL gain. Most items drop
                  here.
               -- Cost:     variance. You'll never have a story to tell
                  about playing logical-only.

  [B] PASSIVE  -- Best for: information without commitment. Sometimes
                  free observations.
               -- Cost:     REL goes down on most days (-1 standard). The
                  character clocks you.

  [C] CHAOTIC  -- Best for: items on crit-success. Funny memories.
                  Highest character payoff. (DICE.)
               -- Cost:     crit-fail is real (~40% baseline). Some
                  chaotics are REL-negative even on success.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[2.02]  The dice math (chaotic only)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Chaotic choices roll a die with a baseline 40% crit-fail rate. On
success: delta multiplied x2, item drop (if any). On crit-fail: delta
multiplied x-1 (so positive deltas become negative, REL gets hit hard,
no item).

+-----------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| + STRATEGY                                                                  |
|                                                                             |
| The chaotic option is the right pick when the _upside_ is what you want.    |
| If a chaotic gives CHR +1 / REL -2 on success, the math says you'll         |
| average roughly CHR +1 / REL -2.8 across many rolls (since crit-fail makes  |
| it WORSE). Don't chaotic-spam for stats; chaotic for the story.             |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------------+

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[2.03]  Inquiries
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Some events have a "[?]" button. Tap before deciding. The character
explains something in their own voice. *FREE.* Doesn't cost AP, doesn't
burn a choice. If you ignore inquiries you'll miss about 30% of the
game's worldbuilding.


===============================================================================
[3.00]  THE CAST
===============================================================================

You meet them in order. Sam is permanent from Day 0; the other three
arrive in narrative time:

  SAM
    Class      : Onboarder
    Domain     : Game systems / meta
    First seen : Day 0 tutorial

  HANA
    Class      : Powerhouse
    Domain     : Fitness, drills, "water levels"
    First seen : Day 1, 5:14 AM text

  KENJI
    Class      : Scholar
    Domain     : Productivity, the ledger, the gold column
    First seen : Day 3, evt 4 (Hana intros him)

  MEI
    Class      : Healer
    Domain     : Cooking, "the line," "the dead"
    First seen : Day 5, evt 1 (Hana brings her over)

The voices are deliberate. Hana CAPS-bursts. Kenji never says "you" in
his own ledger entries -- he says "they" and "their column." Mei's
whole vocabulary is line-cook ("mise," "the line is holding," "the
dead"). The game blind-tests on this -- if you cover the speaker label,
you can identify the character from any line.

     the slowest poison" inquiry -- that exact phrase is in Hana's Day
     3 memory write. The game is showing you that her language is
     consistent across days, which means the LLM context is being
     threaded properly. Small craft, but it's there.

  5. *KENJI IS FUNDED BY HANA.* Day 7 evt-1's "Hana's physical
     requirements are a recurring column I have been quietly funding
     without comment" is the only canonical mention that Kenji is
     paying Hana's expenses. I missed this entirely on run 1.

[9.00]  MINI-REVIEW
===============================================================================

>> WHAT'S WORKING
   --------------
The character voice is the strongest part of the game. I covered the
speaker labels on a printed-out transcript and I could identify Hana
from Kenji from Mei in three lines or fewer, every time. That's craft
discipline. The dialogue rule the team calls "blind-test" is real.

The tier-up cadence is also working. Three reveals in seven days, each
in a different category (memory / opinion / fear / memory), each
landing at evt-(N-1) of its day. The cadence has the rhythm of a
Persona Social Link arc compressed into a week.

The chaos discipline is the load-bearing design choice. Chaotic is not
"the wrong answer that does damage." It's the answer that gets you the
memory. Day 3 evt-5 (eat the agreement card), Day 4 evt-3 (raccoon
fraud), Day 5 evt-5 (deflect Mei's fear via Hana) all reward the
disrespect with something only chaos can deliver. The game means it.

>> WHAT'S STILL WOBBLY
   -------------------
Day 2 is the connecting tissue and it shows. The Kenji-call seam at
evt-2 is the only thing that earns the day. If your first run had a
crit-fail on evt-3 chaotic AND you picked logical or passive on evt-2,
Day 2 is the day you might bounce.

The GLD stat exists in the UI but doesn't move in canon. Sam
acknowledges this if asked, which honors the cosmology, but a player
who notices will wonder.

The Elseworld tier (not covered here -- separate walkthrough) is a
huge surface that the Main Line doesn't introduce. New players who hit
the picker after Day 7 may not know what just happened. Documentation
gap, not a story gap.

>> VERDICT
   -------
9/10 for what it's trying to do. The thing it's trying to do isn't
what most other "AI companion" apps are doing -- those are trying to
be ChatGPT With a Persona. This is trying to be a published RPG module
that happens to be authored by an LLM under a tight gate. The
discipline of the gate is what makes it work. If the gate slips in
v1.0+, the game dies. If it doesn't, this is the only product of its
kind I can name.

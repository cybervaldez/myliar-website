Cover-Line Formats

The front-door one-liner cover — a few lines that preview a character at base REL (their initial, not-yet-warm personality) and hint at the bond that grows as you play. Eight reproducible formats — pick & choose per character, per campaign. Examples use the real cast (drafts — each authored line still gets the Gemini 2nd-opinion vet before it ships). Spec: docs/design/cover-line-formats.md.

The shared rules every variation follows ▾
  1. Base-REL temperature — they don't know you yet; initial personality, never warm. Warmth is the payoff, not the hook.
  2. In-voice — sounds like only this character (quote the real register, don't paraphrase).
  3. MC-POV faceless — 2nd-person perception, never dictates the player's named emotion.
  4. Anticipation, never FOMO — show the shape + "more ahead"; no "X / N" count, no deadline, no dare. Hearsay: describe their intrinsic state, never imply they await/need you ("she's been ready," not "she's waiting").
  5. Honest proportionality — the tease's depth ≤ the real arc. If it isn't authored, the cover doesn't promise it.
  6. The gift is legible — what they give you reads at REL 0 (the value-prop floor).
  7. Grows-with-you — the bond deepens AS autonomy grows; the inversion/peer beat is the gold tease. Preview the shift in KIND (transactional→peer, respect, the process) — not the feeling of warmth; that's the payoff.
  8. Identity grammar — a name is not a drop-in pronoun (§4.5): pronoun tokens for references, [name] for address.
  9. Romance/platonic floor — the Spark hard-gates on romanceEnabled; friction, not flirtation, when off. Root the friction in task/worldview clash, never personal magnetism.
  10. Motif IP-stripped — the genre shape, never an IP name.
  11. displayName routing — never hardcode a title; route through displayName().
  12. 2nd-opinion gate + Riley's gate — Gemini-vet before ratifying; a casual non-fan must want to tap in.
